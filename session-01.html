Session agenda

What happens
in the room.

Every minute is accounted for. Here's exactly how the three hours unfold.

1
0:00 – 0:20
Welcome & the AI reality check
We open by separating fact from fiction. Your team shares what they've heard about AI — we address every concern honestly. No hype, no buzzwords. Just a clear picture of what these tools actually are, what they can't do, and why they won't replace anyone in the room.
Group discussionQ&AMyth busting
2
0:20 – 0:50
Your first live prompt — together
Every person in the room opens ChatGPT or Claude for the first time (or the first time properly). We walk through the interface together, then everyone writes their first prompt. We debrief immediately: what worked, what didn't, and why.
Hands-on practiceIndividual laptopsTrainer-guided
3
0:50 – 1:30
The anatomy of a great prompt
Most AI output is mediocre because most prompts are vague. We teach the three-part structure — Role, Task, Context — that turns generic responses into genuinely useful ones. Each person rewrites their earlier prompt and sees the difference immediately.
Framework teachingLive comparisonPersonal practice

1:30 – 1:45

Spotting bad AI output — the trust filter

Learning outcomes

What your team
walks away knowing.

By the end of Session 1, every person in the room will be able to do these three things independently.

Confident first use
Log in, navigate, and run prompts independently — without needing IT support or a tutorial.
Prompt structure
Write prompts using the Role–Task–Context framework that produces consistently useful output.
Trust calibration
Know which AI outputs to trust directly and which to verify — and have a reliable habit for doing so.
In-session exercises

No slides.
Just doing.

Everything is hands-on. These are the four exercises that make up the core of Session 1.

01
The myth list
Before we start, everyone writes down one thing they believe about AI. We work through every item as a group — confirming, correcting, and clarifying. Sceptics often become the most engaged people in the room.
Opening exercise
02
Prompt before and after
Write a prompt instinctively, see the output, then rewrite it using the Role–Task–Context structure. The difference in quality is usually dramatic — and immediately memorable.
Core skill drill
03
Spot the mistake
We show five AI outputs — some accurate, some subtly wrong, one completely fabricated. Your team identifies the problems. It builds a scepticism muscle that sticks for life.
Critical thinking
04
One real task, done
Each person picks something from their actual to-do list and uses AI to make meaningful progress on it before the session ends. This is the moment it becomes real.
